# Quantum Computing Guide

## Basic Concepts

### Qubits
A qubit (quantum bit) is the fundamental unit of quantum information.

### Superposition
A qubit can exist in a superposition of both 0 and 1 states simultaneously.

### Entanglement
Multiple qubits can be entangled, meaning their states are correlated.

### Measurement
Measuring a qubit collapses its superposition to either 0 or 1.

## Quantum Gates

### Single-Qubit Gates
- **Hadamard (H)**: Creates equal superposition
- **Pauli Gates (X, Y, Z)**: Bit and phase flips
- **Phase Gates (S, T)**: Add phase to qubit

### Multi-Qubit Gates
- **CNOT**: Controlled NOT gate
- **SWAP**: Exchange qubit states
- **Toffoli**: Controlled-controlled NOT

## Algorithms

### Deutsch Algorithm
Determines if a function is balanced or constant.

### Grover Search
Searches unsorted database in O(√N) time.

### Shor Algorithm
Factors large integers efficiently.
